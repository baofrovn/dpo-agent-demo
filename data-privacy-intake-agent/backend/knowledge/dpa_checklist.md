# Data Processing Agreement (DPA) Checklist

## Purpose
This checklist applies to **domestic data sharing or processing cases** where personal data is shared with partners, vendors, or third parties **within Vietnam**.

Use this checklist when:
- Sharing customer data with a domestic partner/vendor
- Outsourcing data processing to a domestic service provider
- Collaborating with domestic third parties that will access personal data
- The recipient/server/access location is within Vietnam

For cross-border cases, use the OTIA Checklist instead.

---

## Required Documents and Information

### 1. Contract or Master Service Agreement (MSA)
- [ ] **Contract/MSA is signed or in draft form**
  - Description: Written agreement between the company and the partner/vendor
  - Why it's needed: Establishes the legal relationship and obligations
  - What to provide: Copy of signed contract or latest draft

### 2. Data Processing Agreement (DPA) or Data Protection Clauses
- [ ] **DPA is included as standalone agreement OR data protection clauses are in the main contract**
  - Description: Specific provisions governing the processing of personal data
  - Why it's needed: Required by data protection laws for any sharing of personal data
  - What to provide: Copy of DPA or highlight relevant clauses in the contract
  - Key requirements: Must cover purpose, data categories, security, retention, sub-processors

### 3. Purpose of Data Processing
- [ ] **Clear business purpose is documented**
  - Description: Specific reason why data needs to be processed/shared
  - Why it's needed: Data can only be used for specified, legitimate purposes
  - What to provide: Written description of:
    - What business problem this solves
    - What the partner/vendor will do with the data
    - How it benefits customers or the business
  - Examples:
    - "To enable customer support services"
    - "To process loan applications"
    - "To send marketing communications"
    - "To perform credit scoring analysis"

### 4. Categories of Personal Data
- [ ] **Specific data fields are listed**
  - Description: Exact data points that will be shared
  - Why it's needed: Must follow data minimization principle (only share what's necessary)
  - What to provide: Detailed list of data fields, such as:
    - Name (first name, last name)
    - Phone number
    - Email address
    - User ID
    - Transaction history
    - Date of birth
    - Address
  - ⚠️ **Important**: If sensitive data (financial, biometric, health) is included, flag it

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities of Parties
- [ ] **Data controller and data processor roles are defined**
  - Description: Clarify who is responsible for what
  - Why it's needed: Legal obligations differ for controllers vs. processors
  - What to provide: Statement of roles:
    - **Data Controller**: Makes decisions about how/why data is processed
    - **Data Processor**: Processes data on behalf of controller
  - Typical scenarios:
    - Company is controller, vendor is processor (most common)
    - Both parties are independent controllers (joint sharing)
    - Company is processor for another entity

### 6. Data Retention Period
- [ ] **How long data will be kept is specified**
  - Description: Maximum time data will be stored by the partner/vendor
  - Why it's needed: Data should not be kept longer than necessary
  - What to provide: Clear retention period:
    - "Data will be retained for [X] months/years"
    - "Data will be deleted within [X] days after service termination"
    - "Data will be retained as long as necessary for [specific purpose]"
  - ⚠️ **Red flag**: "Indefinite retention" or "No specified period"

### 7. Security and Confidentiality Measures
- [ ] **Technical and organizational security measures are documented**
  - Description: How the partner/vendor will protect the data
  - Why it's needed: Required to prevent data breaches
  - What to provide: Description or evidence of:
    - Encryption (in transit and at rest)
    - Access controls and authentication
    - Network security measures
    - Physical security of data centers
    - Employee training on data protection
    - Security certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2, etc.)
  - Minimum acceptable: Encryption + access controls + security policy

### 8. Sub-Processor List (if applicable)
- [ ] **Any sub-contractors who will access data are disclosed**
  - Description: Third parties that the vendor may use
  - Why it's needed: Company must know who else has access to data
  - What to provide:
    - List of sub-processor names
    - What each sub-processor does
    - Where each sub-processor is located
    - Confirmation that sub-processors have equivalent DPAs
  - ⚠️ **Important**: If sub-processor is outside Vietnam, this becomes cross-border case

### 9. Incident Response and Breach Notification
- [ ] **Data breach notification obligations are defined**
  - Description: What happens if there's a security incident
  - Why it's needed: Timely breach notification is legally required
  - What to provide: Contract clauses covering:
    - Vendor must notify company of any data breach
    - Notification timeframe (e.g., within 72 hours)
    - What information must be included in notification
    - Vendor cooperation in breach investigation and remediation
    - Liability and indemnification for breaches

### 10. Data Subject Rights Handling
- [ ] **Mechanism for handling user requests is established**
  - Description: How to handle requests from data subjects (customers)
  - Why it's needed: Users have rights to access, correct, delete their data
  - What to provide: Process for:
    - Access requests (users want to see their data)
    - Correction requests (users want to update data)
    - Deletion requests (users want data removed)
    - Objection to processing
  - Typical clause: "Vendor will assist Company in responding to data subject requests within [X] business days"

### 11. Data Return or Deletion Upon Contract Termination
- [ ] **What happens to data when contract ends is specified**
  - Description: Data disposal process at end of relationship
  - Why it's needed: Vendor should not keep data after service ends
  - What to provide: Contract clause covering:
    - Data will be deleted within [X] days after termination
    - OR data will be returned to company in [specified format]
    - Confirmation of deletion will be provided
    - No copies will be retained (except as required by law)

### 12. Audit Rights
- [ ] **Company has right to audit vendor's data handling**
  - Description: Ability to verify vendor compliance
  - Why it's needed: Allows company to check that vendor follows DPA
  - What to provide: Contract clause stating:
    - Company may audit vendor's data processing activities
    - Frequency of audits (e.g., annually or upon reasonable notice)
    - Vendor will provide necessary documentation
    - Third-party audit reports may be accepted (e.g., SOC 2)

### 13. Evidence of User Consent or Notice (if applicable)
- [ ] **User consent or notice mechanism is documented**
  - Description: How users are informed and consent obtained
  - Why it's needed: Users must be informed about data sharing
  - What to provide:
    - Copy of privacy policy or notice mentioning this data sharing
    - Screenshot of consent checkbox (if applicable)
    - Description of when/how users are notified
  - When required:
    - Sharing data for purposes beyond original collection
    - Sharing with third parties not disclosed in original notice
    - Sharing sensitive personal data

## Risk Assessment Questions

After gathering the above information, assess the risk level:

### High Risk Indicators (Require Extra Scrutiny)
- [ ] Sensitive personal data involved (financial, health, biometric)
- [ ] Large volume of users affected (> 10,000 users)
- [ ] Long retention period (> 5 years)
- [ ] Vendor has no security certifications
- [ ] No clear incident response plan
- [ ] First-time vendor with no track record
- [ ] Critical business function dependency

### Medium Risk Indicators
- [ ] Non-sensitive personal data only
- [ ] Moderate user volume (1,000 - 10,000 users)
- [ ] Standard retention period (1-5 years)
- [ ] Some security measures in place
- [ ] Established vendor with references

### Low Risk Indicators
- [ ] Minimal personal data (e.g., name and email only)
- [ ] Small user volume (< 1,000 users)
- [ ] Short retention period (< 1 year)
- [ ] Strong security certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2)
- [ ] Well-known, reputable vendor
- [ ] Existing DPA template already reviewed

## Submission Checklist

Before submitting for Data Privacy team review, ensure you have:
- [ ] Completed all applicable items above
- [ ] Gathered all required documents
- [ ] Flagged any high-risk indicators
- [ ] Prepared answers to likely follow-up questions
- [ ] Identified decision-makers who can approve this case
- [ ] Estimated timeline for implementation

---

## Common Issues and How to Avoid Them

**Issue 1: "We don't have a DPA yet"**
- Solution: Use company's DPA template, or work with Legal to create one

**Issue 2: "Vendor won't share security details"**
- Solution: Request security certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2) or security questionnaire

**Issue 3: "We're not sure what data will be shared"**
- Solution: Work with engineering team to document exact API fields or data export format

**Issue 4: "This is urgent, can we skip the DPA?"**
- Solution: No. DPA is legally required. Work with Legal for expedited review.

**Issue 5: "The vendor is an international company but has Vietnam office"**
- Solution: Check where data is stored and who accesses it. If outside Vietnam, use OTIA checklist.

---

## Next Steps After Completing Checklist

1. **Submit to Data Privacy Team** with all documents attached
2. **Expect review time**: Allow 5-10 business days for standard cases
3. **Be ready for follow-up questions**: Data Privacy team may need clarifications
4. **Obtain approvals**: May require sign-off from Legal, Compliance, Security teams
5. **Implement controls**: After approval, ensure agreed security measures are in place
6. **Monitor compliance**: Periodically review vendor's adherence to DPA

---

**Remember**: This checklist helps prepare documentation, but final approval comes from Data Privacy legal team.
