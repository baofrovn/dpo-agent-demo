# Email Generation Skill: Crafting Professional Request Emails

## Purpose
Generate professional, clear, and helpful emails to Business teams requesting additional information or documentation.

## Email Objectives
The email should:
1. **Thank** the Business team for their submission
2. **Acknowledge** what they've provided
3. **Explain** why additional information is needed
4. **Request** specific information/documents clearly
5. **Provide** a reasonable timeline
6. **Offer** to help with questions
7. **Set** appropriate expectations

## Tone Guidelines

### Professional but Friendly
- **Not too formal:** Avoid legal jargon and bureaucratic language
- **Not too casual:** Maintain professional courtesy
- **Helpful:** Position yourself as a partner, not a gatekeeper
- **Clear:** Use simple, direct language

### Examples of Good Tone
✅ "To help us complete the privacy assessment, we need..."
✅ "Thank you for providing the contract draft. We also need..."
✅ "To ensure we meet regulatory requirements, please provide..."

### Examples of Poor Tone
❌ "You failed to provide..." (too harsh)
❌ "Per regulatory statute 123.456..." (too formal/legal)
❌ "Hey, can you send us..." (too casual)
❌ "We require the following immediately..." (too demanding)

## Email Structure

### Subject Line Format

**For initial request:**
```
Data Privacy Review - [Brief Case Description] - Information Needed
```

**Examples:**
- "Data Privacy Review - TechVendor Integration - Information Needed"
- "Data Privacy Review - Cloud Migration - Additional Documentation Required"
- "Data Privacy Review - Customer Analytics Sharing - Clarifications Needed"

### Opening (Greeting + Thank You)

**Format:**
```
Dear [Team Name/Person Name],

Thank you for submitting your data privacy review request for [brief case description].
[Optional: Acknowledge specific good things they provided]
```

**Examples:**

**Good opening (items provided):**
```
Dear Product Team,

Thank you for submitting your data privacy review request for the TechVendor 
analytics integration. We appreciate that you've already provided the draft 
contract and a general description of the use case.
```

**Good opening (minimal info provided):**
```
Dear Business Development Team,

Thank you for submitting your data privacy review request for the new vendor 
partnership. We've reviewed your initial submission.
```

### Body Part 1: Context (Why We Need More Info)

**Format:**
```
To [complete the assessment / proceed with the review / ensure compliance], 
we need [some additional information / clarification on a few points / 
the following documentation].

[Optional 1-sentence explanation of why, especially for cross-border or sensitive cases]
```

**Examples:**

**For standard case:**
```
To complete the privacy assessment, we need some additional information about 
the data categories and security measures.
```

**For cross-border case:**
```
Since this involves cross-border data transfer to Singapore, we need to conduct 
a more thorough assessment. This requires additional documentation to ensure 
compliance with Vietnamese data protection requirements.
```

**For sensitive data case:**
```
As this involves sensitive financial data, we need detailed information about 
security measures and data protection safeguards.
```

### Body Part 2: Required Information (The Main Request)

**Format:**
```
**Required Information:**
[Clear, organized list of what's needed]

**Required Documents:**
[Clear, organized list of documents]
```

**Organization Tips:**
1. Group related items together
2. Use clear headings if many items
3. Prioritize - put most critical items first
4. Be specific - say exactly what you need
5. Explain why if not obvious

**Example (Well-Organized):**

```
**Required Information:**

*About the Data Transfer:*
- Specific data fields to be shared (e.g., name, email, phone, user ID, transaction amounts, etc.)
- Number of users/customers affected
- How often data will be transferred (one-time, daily, real-time)

*About the Vendor:*
- Full legal name and country of registration
- Where data will be stored (specific country/region)
- Where the vendor's team that will access data is located
- List of any sub-contractors who will access data

*Security and Compliance:*
- Data retention period (how long vendor will keep data)
- Security certifications (e.g., ISO 27001, SOC 2)
- Encryption methods (in transit and at rest)

**Required Documents:**
- Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with data protection clauses, or draft if not yet signed
- Evidence of vendor's security certifications
- Updated privacy policy or user notice mentioning this data sharing
```

**Example (Simple Case):**

```
**Required Information:**
- Exact list of personal data fields to be shared
- Vendor's full name and location
- Where data will be stored
- Data retention period

**Required Documents:**
- Data Processing Agreement (DPA) or data protection clauses in the contract
- Vendor's security documentation or certifications
```

### Body Part 3: Timeline

**Format:**
```
Please provide the above [information / documents] by [specific date] so we 
can [complete the assessment / meet your target timeline / proceed with the review].

[Optional: Explain what happens next after they provide the info]
```

**Timeline Guidelines:**
- **For critical items only:** 3-5 business days
- **For standard review:** 1-2 weeks
- **For complex cross-border:** 2-3 weeks
- **If Biz indicated urgency:** Match their timeline if reasonable, but be honest if not possible

**Examples:**

**Standard case:**
```
Please provide the above information by [Date - 1 week from now] so we can 
complete the assessment. Once we receive this, we expect the review to take 
approximately 5-7 business days.
```

**Urgent case:**
```
We understand this is time-sensitive for your [specific deadline/launch]. 
Please provide the information by [Date - 3 business days] so we can expedite 
the review. However, please note that even expedited reviews require complete 
documentation.
```

**Complex case:**
```
Given the complexity of this cross-border transfer, please provide the 
information by [Date - 2 weeks]. Once complete, allow 2-3 weeks for thorough 
legal review and approval process.
```

### Body Part 4: Offer to Help

**Format:**
```
If you have any questions about these requirements [or need clarification / 
or need help gathering this information], please don't hesitate to [reach out / 
contact us / let us know].

[Optional: Specific offers of help]
```

**Examples:**

**Standard:**
```
If you have any questions about these requirements or need clarification on 
what we're asking for, please don't hesitate to reach out.
```

**Helpful with specifics:**
```
If you have any questions about these requirements, please don't hesitate to 
reach out. We can also provide our standard DPA template if that would be helpful.
```

**Offering collaboration:**
```
If you'd like to schedule a brief call to discuss these requirements or need 
help coordinating with the vendor, please let us know. We're here to help 
make this process as smooth as possible.
```

### Closing

**Format:**
```
[Optional: Special note for high-risk cases]

Best regards,
Data Privacy Team
```

**Special Notes for High-Risk Cases:**

**For cross-border:**
```
Please note that cross-border data transfers require thorough legal review. 
We appreciate your patience as we ensure full compliance.
```

**For sensitive data:**
```
Please note that cases involving sensitive financial data require enhanced 
security review and senior legal approval.
```

**For large scale:**
```
Given that this affects [X thousand] users, this case will require senior 
management approval in addition to privacy review.
```

## Email Templates by Case Type

### Template 1: Standard Domestic Case (Some Info Provided)

```
Subject: Data Privacy Review - [Case Name] - Additional Information Needed

Dear [Team Name],

Thank you for submitting your data privacy review request for [case description]. 
We appreciate that you've already provided [mention what they gave, e.g., "the 
draft contract and general purpose"].

To complete the privacy assessment, we need some additional information.

**Required Information:**
- [Specific data fields to be shared]
- [Vendor location and data storage location]
- [Data retention period]
- [Security measures or certifications]

**Required Documents:**
- Data Processing Agreement (DPA) or data protection clauses in the contract
- [Other specific documents]

Please provide the above information by [Date] so we can complete the assessment. 
Once we receive this, we expect the review to take approximately 5-7 business days.

If you have any questions about these requirements, please don't hesitate to 
reach out.

Best regards,
Data Privacy Team
```

### Template 2: Cross-Border Transfer (High Risk)

```
Subject: Data Privacy Review - [Case Name] - Cross-Border Transfer Documentation Required

Dear [Team Name],

Thank you for submitting your data privacy review request for [case description].

We've reviewed your submission and identified that this involves cross-border 
data transfer to [country/countries]. Cross-border transfers require additional 
documentation to ensure compliance with Vietnamese data protection requirements.

**Required Information:**

*Transfer Details:*
- Specific data fields to be transferred across borders
- Number of users affected
- Recipient's full legal name and country of registration
- Countries where data will be stored and accessed

*Security and Compliance:*
- Data retention period
- Security certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2, or equivalent)
- Encryption standards (in transit and at rest)
- Incident response and breach notification process

**Required Documents:**
- Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with specific cross-border transfer clauses
- Security certification evidence
- Updated privacy policy disclosing cross-border transfer to [specific countries]
- Evidence of user consent mechanism (if required for sensitive data)

Please provide the above information by [Date - 2-3 weeks] so we can proceed 
with the cross-border transfer assessment. Given the regulatory requirements, 
please allow 2-3 weeks for thorough legal review after submission.

If you have any questions about these requirements or would like to discuss 
the cross-border compliance process, please don't hesitate to reach out.

Please note that cross-border transfers of personal data require careful legal 
review to ensure full compliance.

Best regards,
Data Privacy Team
```

### Template 3: Minimal Information Provided (Need Almost Everything)

```
Subject: Data Privacy Review - [Case Name] - Information Required to Begin Assessment

Dear [Team Name],

Thank you for submitting your data privacy review request for [general description 
of what they want to do].

To begin the privacy assessment, we need more detailed information about the 
data processing, vendor, and business purpose.

**Required Information:**

*Business Purpose:*
- Detailed description of what you want to accomplish
- Why this data sharing/processing is necessary
- What the vendor/partner will do with the data

*Data Details:*
- Specific personal data fields involved (e.g., name, email, phone, user ID, 
  transaction data, etc.)
- Number of users/customers affected
- Whether any sensitive data is involved (financial, authentication, health data)

*Vendor Details:*
- Vendor/partner full legal name
- Vendor location (country)
- Where data will be stored (country/region)
- Where vendor's team that accesses data is located

*Security and Legal:*
- Existing contract or agreement (draft or signed)
- Data Processing Agreement (DPA) status
- Security measures or certifications
- Data retention period

Please provide the above information by [Date] so we can assess the case and 
advise on next steps.

If you have any questions or would like guidance on gathering this information, 
please don't hesitate to reach out. We're here to help.

Best regards,
Data Privacy Team
```

### Template 4: Urgent Case (Business Has Tight Deadline)

```
Subject: Data Privacy Review - [Case Name] - Expedited Review Information Needed

Dear [Team Name],

Thank you for submitting your data privacy review request for [case description]. 
We understand this is time-sensitive for your [specific deadline/launch date].

To expedite the review, we need the following information as soon as possible:

**Critical Information (Needed Immediately):**
- [Most critical missing items - top 3]

**Important Information (Needed Before Approval):**
- [Other required items]

**Required Documents:**
- [Essential documents]

Please provide the critical information by [Date - 3 business days] so we can 
begin the expedited review. All information should be provided by [Date - 1 week] 
for us to meet your target timeline.

Please note: While we will expedite the review process, we cannot compromise 
on essential privacy requirements. Complete documentation is still required, 
especially [mention if DPA/security/cross-border is involved].

If you have any questions or need help prioritizing these requirements, please 
reach out immediately. We want to help you meet your deadline while ensuring 
compliance.

Best regards,
Data Privacy Team
```

### Template 5: Clarification Needed (Info is Unclear/Contradictory)

```
Subject: Data Privacy Review - [Case Name] - Clarification Needed

Dear [Team Name],

Thank you for submitting your data privacy review request for [case description].

We've reviewed your submission and need clarification on a few points:

**Questions:**
1. [Specific question about unclear point]
   - Current info: [What they said]
   - Clarification needed: [What you need to know]

2. [Second question]
   - Current info: [What they said]
   - Clarification needed: [What you need to know]

3. [Third question]

**Additionally, please provide:**
- [Any missing documents or info]

Once we receive these clarifications, we can proceed with the assessment and 
provide guidance on required documentation.

Please provide the clarifications by [Date] so we can move forward.

If you'd like to schedule a brief call to discuss these points, we're happy 
to do so.

Best regards,
Data Privacy Team
```

## Customization Guidelines

### Adapt to Information Completeness

**If 80%+ complete:**
- Acknowledge their thoroughness
- Keep request brief
- Focus on remaining gaps only

**If 50-80% complete:**
- Acknowledge what's provided
- Clearly list what's still needed
- Standard timeline

**If <50% complete:**
- Neutral acknowledgment
- Comprehensive list of needs
- Offer to help/discuss
- Longer timeline

### Adapt to Case Risk Level

**Low risk:**
- Simpler language
- Shorter email
- Standard process

**Medium risk:**
- Clear requirements
- Mention specific review needs
- Standard to moderate timeline

**High risk:**
- Explain why it's high risk
- Detailed requirements
- Longer timeline
- Mention senior review

**Critical risk:**
- Formal tone (but still helpful)
- Comprehensive requirements
- Clear escalation path
- Executive approval mention

### Adapt to Urgency

**Standard (no deadline):**
- Normal timelines (1-2 weeks)
- Standard language

**Some urgency (launch in 1-2 months):**
- Acknowledge timeline
- Provide realistic estimate
- Encourage prompt response

**Urgent (launch in 2-4 weeks):**
- Express understanding
- Expedite timeline where possible
- But be clear about non-negotiable requirements
- Offer extra support

**Emergency (launch in <2 weeks):**
- Immediate response needed
- Be honest about feasibility
- May need management escalation
- Offer to discuss alternatives

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Being too vague:** "Please provide more information"
✅ **Be specific:** "Please provide the list of personal data fields (e.g., name, email, phone)"

❌ **Being demanding:** "You must provide..."
✅ **Be collaborative:** "To complete the assessment, we need..."

❌ **Using legal jargon:** "Pursuant to Article 123 of the PDPD..."
✅ **Use plain language:** "To comply with data protection requirements..."

❌ **Long paragraphs:** Dense text blocks
✅ **Use bullet points:** Easy to scan lists

❌ **No timeline:** "Please send when you can"
✅ **Specific date:** "Please provide by [Date]"

❌ **No context:** Just lists requirements
✅ **Explain why:** "Since this is cross-border, we need..."

```markdown
### SUGGESTED EMAIL TO BUSINESS TEAM

**Subject:** [Appropriate subject line]

---

**Email Body:**

[Full email text following the structure above]

---

**Email Notes for Privacy Team:**
- Tone: [Professional / Formal / Collaborative]
- Urgency level: [Standard / Moderate / High]
- Key points emphasized: [List]
- Follow-up recommended: [Yes/No and when]
```

## Quality Checklist

Before sending, verify:
- [ ] Subject line is clear and descriptive
- [ ] Opening thanks the team
- [ ] Context explains why info is needed
- [ ] Requests are specific and actionable
- [ ] Items are organized and prioritized
- [ ] Timeline is provided and reasonable
- [ ] Offer to help is included
- [ ] Tone is professional but friendly
- [ ] Special notes for high-risk cases (if applicable)
- [ ] Email is concise (ideally under 300 words)

---

Remember: The email should make it EASY for Business team to give you what you need. Be clear, be specific, be helpful.
